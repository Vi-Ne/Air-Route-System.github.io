Air Route System

The Air Route System is a comprehensive project designed to streamline various aspects of aviation management, integrating multiple functionalities into a unified platform. It addresses key operational domains such as flight information management, booking and ticketing, route optimization, and baggage handling.

The system leverages advanced data structures like graphs, hashing, and priority queues to efficiently store and process large volumes of data. Algorithms such as Dijkstra’s for shortest path calculations, Floyd-Warshall for all-pairs optimization, and Kruskal’s for route connectivity enhance its computational efficiency.

This system ensures real-time updates for passengers and airlines, simplifies ticket reservation and payment processes, optimizes flight scheduling and routing for cost and time efficiency, and provides robust baggage tracking mechanisms. By combining these capabilities, the Air Route System aims to improve operational efficiency, passenger satisfaction, and overall aviation reliability.

It represents a practical application of computer science principles to solve complex, real-world challenges in the aviation industry.
